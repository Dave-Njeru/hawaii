Activities To Do in Hawaii

1. Hiking at Wailua River

This five-hour guided tour is a wonderful waterfall adventure. It combines idyllic paddling on the Wailua River and at times, depending on weather conditions, a moderate challenging hike to a remote bridal-veil waterfall. The Wailua River became the center of activity for early Hawaiians and is known as one of Hawaii's two most sacred places. It is said, at night, that one can still hear ancient drums thunder in concert with the nearby surf.

2. Lahaina Banyan Court Park

Cool off with a shave ice in the Lahaina Banyan Court Park under the shady branches of the oldest tree. Hawaii's oldest non-native arboreal giant arrived from India in 1873. William Owen Smith, Maui sheriff, planted it to commemorate the 50th anniversary of the first American Protestant mission here. It's now 12 meters tall with a half-kilometer canopy and roots that drape across the ground. It's filled with bough-swinging children or chattering birds, depending on the time of day.

3. Private Off-Road Adventure Tour

Take a break from the beach for an off-road adventure in the Oahu countryside on this private tour. With a guide at the wheel of 4-wheel-drive vehicle, you'll pass by a variety of features including a hidden cave, filming locations, ranch animals, and historical sites from World War II. Visit a lookout point for views of the hilly landscape, and stop for a picnic and private time to relax.
